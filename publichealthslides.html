The Public Health Angle

This is not a new cost.
It is a smarter use of what you already spend.

Drug and alcohol commissioning budgets are under sustained pressure. But a significant portion of current spend is reactive — managing crisis presentations, revolving-door hospitalisations, and repeat engagement with individuals who never address the root cause of their addiction.

OTOS Continuity proposes a different model: identify and treat the underlying driver — in this case, undiagnosed ADHD — and watch the reactive spend reduce. Not through cuts. Through better-connected, earlier, smarter intervention.

23–45% of adults in addiction treatment have undiagnosed ADHD. Treating it reduces both ADHD symptoms and substance use outcomes. The research is clear. The question is why this cohort is still not being systematically identified.

— Royal College of Psychiatrists, 2022 & NICE NG87

Current spend pattern

With OTOS Continuity

How It Works

OTOS Continuity does not replace existing services — it connects them, and maintains the individual's thread through every organisation they touch.

Evidence Base

The research already says what we know is true.

Key Supporting Evidence
RCPsych 2022
"There is more benefit than risk from treating ADHD in patients with substance use disorder — for both ADHD and substance use symptom reduction."
NICE NG87
NICE guidance supports ADHD assessment and treatment in the context of active substance misuse — coexisting ADHD is specifically addressed.
Progress in Mind 2022
"Lisdexamfetamine appeared to reduce craving in cocaine misuse. No evidence of misuse was found in properly supervised patients."
NCBI 2024
"People with ADHD have a significantly raised risk of drug and alcohol misuse — and this complicates ADHD diagnosis and management in standard clinical settings."

CGL Nottingham — a precedent already set. Change Grow Live, one of OTOS Continuity's core delivery partners, trialled an ADHD identification and referral pathway in Nottingham in 2024–25. The results demonstrated that frontline addiction workers, with light-touch training, could screen for and identify likely ADHD in their existing caseloads — and that warm referral into assessment services produced significantly better engagement than standard referral routes. This is not a new idea being tested from scratch. The groundwork is already being laid by partners already in this ecosystem.

The Pre-Pilot

50 people.
A proof point that changes the commissioning conversation.

OTOS has already identified 877 individuals in the Cambridge area matching the target cohort profile. The pre-pilot selects 50 high-priority individuals — with partners engaged and referral pathways ready.

The aim is a 12-month demonstrator that produces an outcomes-based evidence report suitable for ICB commissioning consideration. This is how we move from promising model to funded pathway.

What We Are Asking For

Five things.
Starting with a conversation.

  • Guidance on budget alignment — where within existing drug and alcohol commissioning spend could this model be funded or supported, even partially
  • Introductions to the right people — ICB commissioning leads, NHS colleagues, or VCSE partners who can accelerate governance and pathway alignment
  • Endorsement or support in principle — from Public Health for the pre-pilot demonstrator, to support conversations with NHS commissioners
  • Data intelligence — any data, intelligence or mapping relevant to the identified cohort that could sharpen the pilot targeting
  • A route to formal commissioning — the longer-term goal is a permanently funded, joined-up ADHD-addiction pathway. Public Health endorsement is a key step in building that case

A note on lived experience: OTOS Continuity was developed by someone who personally went through addiction services, mental health crisis, and revolving-door care — before finally receiving an ADHD diagnosis privately and being prescribed Elvanse. The transformation was immediate. The model is not theoretical. It is built from direct, lived understanding of exactly where the system fails — and what genuinely joined-up, properly supported care can achieve. That experience informs every aspect of the pathway design.
