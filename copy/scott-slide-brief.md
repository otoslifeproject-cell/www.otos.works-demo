# Scott slide brief

## Purpose
This is a short pre-read for Scott Davidson.
It should read like a premium 6-slide deck on one page.
It is not the full OTOS site.
It is not a partner pack.
It is not a technical specification.

## Non-negotiables
- Keep to 6 sections only
- One core idea per section
- Minimal copy
- Premium editorial design
- Strong local/public-health relevance
- Clear savings logic without overclaiming
- Low-integration, low-disruption positioning
- Human-in-control throughout

## Slide 1 — Problem
### Must communicate
Untreated or unresolved ADHD can drive addiction and chaos for a significant cohort.
The failure is not only in treatment access; it is in the space between services while the real condition remains unresolved.

### Best current direction
Headline:
When ADHD is left waiting, addiction does not arrive quietly — it brings chaos with it.

Support:
For a significant and identifiable group of adults, addiction is not the whole clinical picture. The failure is not in the care. It is in the space between services while the real condition remains unresolved.

Optional support line:
The system keeps treating the crisis, but not always the condition driving it.

## Slide 2 — Founder truth
### Must communicate
This is built from lived system failure, not theory.
The founder journey exposed the missing continuity layer.

### Best current direction
Headline:
I am not adjacent to this problem. I lived it.

Support:
Six years in the system. Residential rehab. Re-presentation. Still waiting for the medication review that never came. OTOS was built from what that journey exposed: nobody could see the whole thread, and nothing held it between services.

Allowed proof cards:
- Three months in residential rehab cost £90,000.
- The turning point came after the system.
- This is why the continuity engine exists.

## Slide 3 — Economic rationale
### Must communicate
A modest local test could be worth funding because the current pattern is expensive and late.

### Best current direction
Headline:
£90,000 spent late on one person. £91,520 to test earlier continuity for fifty.

Support:
This is not a request to fund a rollout. It is a request to fund the question: can a small, governed continuity layer reduce costly late failure across a high-risk cohort before crisis returns?

Caution line:
This is not a claim of guaranteed savings. It is a bounded test of whether local downstream cost can be reduced if continuity improves earlier.

Closing line:
I am not asking anyone to fund a rollout. I am asking the system to fund the question.

## Slide 4 — What OTOS is
### Must communicate
OTOS is a non-clinical continuity layer around existing services.

### Best current direction
Headline:
A non-clinical continuity layer around existing services.

Support:
OTOS logs handoffs, generates transition receipts, monitors continuity touchpoints, and surfaces early drift signals for human review. The clinical action stays with the service. OTOS holds the thread.

Guardrail chips:
- Not an EPR
- Not diagnosis
- Not prescribing
- No EPR write-back
- Human-in-control throughout

Bottom line:
Touchpoints, not records.

## Slide 5 — The demonstrator
### Must communicate
This is a 12-week demonstrator for people most likely to be lost in the gap.

### Best current direction
Headline:
A 12-week demonstrator for the people most likely to be lost in the gap.

Support:
This is continuity layer only. OTOS does not treat ADHD. It creates the receipted thread around adults with co-occurring ADHD and substance misuse so services can see whether the pathway is actually holding.

Four boxes only:
- Cohort design
- What gets tested
- What this produces
- Why this matters

Best support line:
Same person. Two silos. One continuity thread.

Community line allowed:
Hospital to community should be continuity, not a drop-off point.

## Slide 6 — The ask
### Must communicate
This is a route-finding conversation, not a national sales pitch.

### Best current direction
Headline:
Help me move this forward the right way.

Support:
I am not asking you to buy a national solution. I am asking for your advice and support on the cleanest route to move a small, governed, low-integration local demonstrator forward.

Four boxes only:
