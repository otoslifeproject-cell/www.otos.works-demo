Low integration. Low disruption. High clarity.

You're already doing the hard part. You refer. You signpost. You discharge. The gap isn't in your service — it's in the space after the door closes. OTOS sits in that space. Quietly. Without touching your systems or your records. From this point on, this portal refers to it as Continuity.

— “There should be a single, accessible front door.” — Independent…

1

Anonymous at first contact

When someone comes to your service, a staff member taps Register touchpoint or scans a QR code. That's it. OTOS creates a secure, anonymous journey ID.

No name. No date of birth. No diagnosis. No extra form to fill in.
2

Touchpoints, not records

As someone moves through services and community support, each provider can note that a touchpoint happened against the same journey ID:

Attended. Missed. Contacted. Referred. Stabilising.
3

Early warning, no extra work

OTOS notices when the pattern changes — repeated missed contacts, ping-pong referrals, long silences. Your team gets an alert. You decide what to do. OTOS never makes clinical decisions. It just makes sure the question "where did they go?" has an answer.
4

Reporting your manager will actually read

Team leads and service managers can see where journeys stall, where drop-off is happening, and where continuity is working. Aggregate, anonymous signals — no individual patient data. No GDPR headache.

Nothing changes in how you work. Your clinical decisions stay yours — always.

OTOS surfaces signals. The partner team makes the decision.
