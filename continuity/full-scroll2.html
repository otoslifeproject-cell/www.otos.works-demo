How it works

How OTOS works — in four steps anyone can follow.

It’s not a new clinical system. OTOS sits lightly on top of what you already run: anonymous touchpoints, human decisions only, and one journey thread so teams can see whether support actually connects — not just whether it was referred.

  1. 1
    Touchpoint

    Logged in seconds — no EPR write-back

    At the bedside, in the hub, or at the front door: staff open OTOS and record that a contact happened (when, where, what kind). The person can stay anonymous. OTOS keeps a tokenised signal — not a duplicate clinical record. Nothing writes back to your EPR.

  2. 2
    Human review

    The right team sees the signal

    That touchpoint surfaces in the partner workflow that needs to act (ward, VCSE, primary care — configured in the pilot). OTOS does not auto-discharge, auto-refer, or decide care. People choose outreach, referral, or no further action.

  3. 3
    Follow-through

    See whether the handoff actually landed

    When a referral or handoff is made, OTOS tracks whether the next step happened — as signals (e.g. attended, callback made), not by exposing another service’s notes. You can answer: did continuity hold, or did we lose them in the gap?

  4. 4
    One thread

    Every step stitches onto the same journey

    Approved touchpoints add to one thread over time. Reporting shows the shape of the pathway — where people flow, where they stall, where drop-off repeats. Same pilot stance: low integration into existing systems, high clarity for governance and improvement.

When a partner flags a suspected ADHD indicator, OTOS carries that signal to the referral route. NHS Right to Choose is already available — every service provider can use it. QbTest is the fast-track assessment tool at the end of that pathway.
