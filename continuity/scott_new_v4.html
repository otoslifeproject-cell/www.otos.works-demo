Slide 1 · ADHD opener

When ADHD is left waiting, addiction does not arrive quietly — it brings chaos with it.

For a significant and identifiable group of adults, addiction is not the whole clinical picture. The crisis often grows while the real condition remains unresolved.

Slide 2 · The chaos

This is what waiting can become.

When ADHD is left untreated for too long, addiction can emerge as a way of coping with overwhelm, under-stimulation, emotional intensity, and executive dysfunction. It does not solve those problems; it compounds them. Sleep becomes erratic, routines collapse, money becomes harder to manage, work becomes unstable, relationships come under strain, and shame drives further secrecy and isolation. What begins as self-medication can quickly become a cycle of short-term relief followed by longer-term damage.

The system keeps treating the crisis, but not always the condition driving it.

Slide 4 · Economic rationale

£90,000 spent late on one person. £91,520 to test earlier continuity for fifty.

This is not a request to fund a rollout. It is a request to fund the question: can a small, governed continuity layer reduce costly late failure across a high-risk local cohort before crisis returns?

This is not a claim of guaranteed savings. It is a bounded local test of whether earlier continuity can reduce downstream cost across health, addiction, and wider public-service demand.

I am not asking the system to fund a rollout. I am asking it to fund the question.

Slide 5 · What OTOS is

A non-clinical continuity layer for the people most likely to be lost in the gap.

OTOS sits around existing services. It logs touchpoints, generates transition receipts, monitors continuity status, and surfaces early drift for human review. The clinical action stays with the service. OTOS holds the thread.

Same person. Two silos. One continuity thread.

Not an EPRNot diagnosisNot prescribingNo EPR write-backHuman-in-control throughout

Cohort design

Up to 50 adults already touching addiction or mental-health services whose pathway is vulnerable to silent disengagement.

What gets tested

QR or link entry, tokenised ID, short non-clinical micro check-ins, transition receipts, drift detection, and human review.

What this produces

Decision-grade evidence on follow-through visibility, early drift detection, service burden, and whether a continuity layer is worth scaling.
