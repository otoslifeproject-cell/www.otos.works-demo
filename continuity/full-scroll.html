ROUTE A — ADDENBROOKE’S DISCHARGE

When someone is at their lowest — OTOS is already there.

Frontline staff log a touchpoint in seconds. The system does the rest.

CPFT on-site alcohol liaison nurse (Addenbrooke’s)

Many patients aren’t already known to alcohol services: they were admitted for something else, then went into withdrawal and needed a detox while they stayed on the ward. The CPFT alcohol liaison nurse on-site visits after that detox trigger to talk about drinking and signpost next steps — including people in the “middle, no route” gap with dual diagnosis when standard options have run out. With OTOS, they log a continuity touchpoint in seconds before the patient leaves.

L2 — Named strategic connection

QbTest

When a partner flags a suspected ADHD indicator, OTOS carries that signal to the referral route. NHS Right to Choose is already available — every service provider can use it. QbTest is the fast-track assessment tool at the end of that pathway.

  • Partner flags indicator — OTOS carries the signal
  • NHS Right to Choose — the existing standard pathway
  • QbTest: objective, CE-marked ADHD assessment
  • Goal: 50 people to faster diagnosis, not years of waiting
L3 — Named strategic connection

Joy (JoyConnect)

Joy gets people to the right front door. OTOS makes sure they follow through. A natural Phase 3 extension as OTOS expands into Neighbourhood Health and the 250 planned NHS community hubs across England.

  • Joy: GP-level signposting, primary care workflow
  • OTOS: continuity after the signpost lands
  • 250 NHS community hubs — the expansion context
  • Phase 1 requires no Joy integration
250 community hubs

Where the front door meets follow-through.
