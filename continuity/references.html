References

References and sources

This is a plain-English source list for the Continuity portal. It is here so partners can quickly verify what is being quoted and what is being assumed. These links are references, not endorsements.

How to use this page: if you see a quote or claim in the portal (or in a pilot conversation), use the sections below to find the source link. If you think something is missing, we will add it.

Core policy and guidance (the “why”)

These are the main documents that support the idea that continuity, joined-up working, and “no wrong door” delivery are already expected in UK health and care.

NHS England — Independent ADHD Taskforce Report (Part 1 and Part 2)
Background, pressures, and recommended direction of travel for ADHD care in England.
https://www.england.nhs.uk/mental-health/adhd-taskforce/
ADHD Joined-up care Service pressure
DHSC — Co-occurring mental health and substance use delivery framework
National framing for co-occurring need and practical expectations for delivery partnerships.
https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/co-occurring-substance-misuse-and-mental-health-conditions-delivery-framework
Dual diagnosis No wrong door Partnership delivery
NICE guideline NG87 — Attention deficit hyperactivity disorder
NICE recommendations for ADHD recognition, diagnosis, and management.
https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng87
NICE ADHD
Royal College of Psychiatrists — CR235 (ADHD in adults)
Good practice guidance for adult ADHD services and pathways.
https://www.rcpsych.ac.uk/.../cr235

Piloting and evaluation (what “good pilot feedback” looks like)

These sources support the idea that pilots are meant to reduce uncertainty, understand workflow impact, and create decision-grade evidence before wider adoption.

NHS England — How-to guide for measurement for improvement (Outcome / Process / Balancing)
Common NHS approach to choosing measures that are safe and realistic.
https://www.england.nhs.uk/.../How-to-Guide-for-Measurement-for-Improvement.pdf
KPIs Balancing measures Improvement
