Problem opening
Problem text

When ADHD is left waiting, addiction does not arrive quietly — it brings chaos with it.

When ADHD is left untreated for too long, addiction can emerge as a way of coping with overwhelm, under-stimulation, emotional intensity and executive dysfunction. But addiction does not solve those problems; it compounds them. In general terms, it can bring chaos into every part of a person’s life: sleep becomes erratic, routines collapse, money becomes harder to manage, work becomes unstable, relationships come under strain, and shame drives further secrecy and isolation. What may begin as self-medication can quickly become a cycle of short-term relief followed by longer-term damage, where the person is no longer simply waiting for help — they are fighting to stop everything falling apart.

How this works in the CGL × CPFT pilot.

Stage 1 is specific: the addiction pathway and the mental-health pathway share continuity signals only. OTOS logs touchpoints, handoff receipts, and drift markers on one encrypted token — no shared case notes, no EPR write-back, human decisions only.

  1. 1
    CGL / CPFT touchpoint

    A clinician logs one continuity event in seconds

    When a key contact happens, the pathway owner logs that contact happened (time, service, event type). The person can remain anonymous. OTOS stores a tokenised continuity signal, not a duplicate clinical record.

  2. 2
    Partner visibility

    CGL and CPFT see the same continuity thread state

    The signal appears in the agreed partner view. Teams can see follow-through status and handoff receipts on the same token. OTOS does not auto-refer, discharge, or make care decisions.

  3. 3
    Handoff receipts

    You can tell if the handoff landed or drifted

    When onward support is agreed, OTOS tracks landing vs drift as continuity signals (attended, missed, callback, due). You can answer quickly: did this person progress, or disappear between services?

  4. 4
    Governance-safe

    One encrypted thread, no shared records

    Every approved touchpoint adds to one journey thread. Reporting shows pathway shape and repeat drop-off patterns. Same stance throughout: low integration, low disruption, high clarity.

1. First contact is logged by the pathway owner

ROUTE C — GP → WORKWELL (JOY · CPSL MIND)

Official referral, informal handoffs — continuity still has to hold.

Signed off on a fit note with mental-health and addiction needs: primary care signposts into WorkWell Cambridge City via Joy; CPSL Mind Work and Health Coaches help people access the right mix of support (including recovery coaching and connections to counselling and substance-misuse services). OTOS carries anonymous signals when blocks of support end but the thread doesn’t.

Priya, counsellor (WorkWell pathway)

Priya is working with someone who reached WorkWell after a GP-led signpost while signed off work — co-occurring addiction, depression, and anxiety on the fit note. They accessed WorkWell through Joy; they chose a block of counselling (recovery coaching was also available). When counselling ends, the next steps with primary care and onward services can go quiet — even when risk hasn’t. Priya cannot see other organisations’ records, and does not need to. OTOS shows whether agreed next steps happened and flags drift, as signals only.

ROUTE D — DUAL PATHWAY (CGL · CPFT)

Same person. Two silos. One continuity thread.

Addiction and mental health often meet the same life at different doors — without a shared record. OTOS does not merge EPRs; it carries an encrypted, anonymised journey token in the continuity layer so both sides can see follow-through, not each other’s notes.

Dual diagnosis — pilot framing

One doorway: someone in addiction treatment where ADHD is suspected. Another: someone in mental health care where substance use is escalating. Too often that means duplicated appointments, duplicated contacts, and overload for the person in the middle. OTOS unites the silos at the continuity layer only — touchpoints and handoff receipts on the same token, with human review and governance agreed in the pilot.

Express gateway (Phase 1 proposal): start with a narrow posture between these two providers — the addiction silo (e.g. CGL / CRS) and the mental health silo (CPFT) — before widening. Batch or correlation analytics would be aggregate and pre-agreed only, not automatic “match everyone” queries. Whether both sides tokenise every contact on day one is a governance and cohort design choice, not a technical default.
